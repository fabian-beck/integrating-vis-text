Introduction

A data visualization is a visual representation of information encoded in geometric primitives that we perceive, process, and interpret to consume the information.

A text is a visual representation of information encoded in characters that we perceive, process, and interpret to consume the information.

The way of encoding might be different but visualization and text are hence similar in the sense that they both convey information to a human reader. Often, they are combined and complement each other as part of documents, user interfaces, presentation slides and the like. While this complementing similarity is certainly not a new observation, it still remains surprising to me how little the combination and close integration of the two modalities haven been investigated. With this book, I want to contribute to closing this gap.
